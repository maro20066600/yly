About YLY Competition

YLY Competition is a national youth initiative launched by the Ministry of Youth and Sports as part of the "YLY ‚Äì Youth Leading Youth" volunteer program. Since its inception, the competition has served as a platform for young people across all Egyptian governorates to showcase their talents, leadership skills, and creativity through a variety of competitive events. Now in its third edition, YLY Competition continues to expand, attracting over 5,000 participants and engaging a digital audience of more than half a million people through social media.

Our Mission

YLY Competition is designed to:

  • Empower young talents by providing them with a platform to demonstrate their skills.
  • Promote leadership and innovation among Egyptian youth.
  • Encourage teamwork and collaboration across different regions.
  • Support community-driven initiatives that contribute to social and cultural development.
  • Enhance digital engagement by leveraging social media to showcase youth creativity.

Competition Structure

YLY Competition is conducted in two formats: Online and Offline. The offline phase includes on-ground competitions held in different governorates, while the online phase allows participants to submit entries remotely, reaching a wider audience.

Achievements & Impact

Since its launch, YLY Competition has successfully:

  • Discovered and nurtured new talents in various fields.
  • Provided national-level recognition to young achievers.
  • Created a network of future leaders who continue to contribute to their communities.
  • Strengthened digital presence, with thousands engaging with the competition online.

Annual Recognition & Awards

Each year, the top-performing governorates and individuals receive official recognition, awards, and the YLY Competition Trophy. The latest edition saw Suez, Beni Suef, and Alexandria securing the top three positions in the national ranking.
